Statement of Faith and Practices

The Family Fellowship Church of Christ believes in the Holy Trinity of God, the Virgin Birth of Jesus Christ, and the Bible as the infallible word of God and as final authority in all matters of sin, salvation, eternal life, and relational issues here on earth.

The Church takes its stand on basic Christian truths which belong to God's people at all times. There are, however, certain distinctive teachings and practices that are strongly emphasized. They are stated as follows:

a) Authority of Scripture

The ultimate and final authority of the Bible in all matters of faith and conduct. The Bible teaches all that is necessary for our salvation (2 Timothy 3:16) and therefore, qualification for inheritance of God's kingdom.

b) New Birth Through Christ

New birth for new creation through the atoning blood, death and resurrection of Jesus Christ (John 3:1-7, Mt. 26:26–29, 2 Cor. 5:17). Man is born again by the Grace of God (Ephesians 2:8–9), through Faith (Acts 16:31) and repentance (Acts 3:19). This is the only way one becomes a bona-fide member of the Body of Christ referred to as the church in the New Testament. Family Fellowship Church of Christ demands the same for admission to full membership.

c) Indwelling of the Holy Spirit

The indwelling of the Holy Spirit in the life of the believer (John 14). The evidence of His indwelling is the fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23) with attendant spiritual gifts (1 Corinthians 12) for the edification of the believers and the church as the body of Christ.

d) Baptism of Believers

Baptism of believers by immersion signifying death to the old life of sin (2 Corinthians 5:17, Romans 6:1-2) and resurrection to a new life in Christ. Baptism is not a means of salvation nor a qualification for membership to Family Fellowship Church of Christ.

e) Holiness of Life and Conduct

Holiness of life and conduct (Romans 6:1, 1 Peter 1:14-25, 1 John 2:1-6, 3:4-10). Walking the talk and preaching what we live. Family Fellowship Church of Christ believes that character supersedes works, however great.

f) Divine Healing

Divine healing of the sick according to the will of God through prayers and faith (James 5:13–16, Psalms 103:2-5, Isaiah 53:5).

g) Unity of Believers

Unity of all born-again believers as one body in Jesus Christ (Galatians 3:28, Ephesians 4:13–16, Psalms 133, John 17:21–33, 1 Corinthians 12:13–26).

h) Kingdom of God

The present reality of the Kingdom of God, the rule and reign of God in the hearts of believers (Luke 17:20-21, Romans 14:17). It is not an earthly Kingdom (John 18:36).

i) Second Coming of Christ

The second coming of Jesus Christ at a time of God's own appointing to judge the world, to punish the wicked and to reward the righteous (Matthew 24:36, 25:46, 1 Thessalonians 4:13–5:2).

j) Restitution in Reconciliation

Restitutionary provision in reconciliation process as evidenced in the Bible (Luke 19:8-10, Acts 23:1-5, Philemon 7:21, Hebrews 2:17-18, 2 Corinthians 5:18–20, Ezekiel 45:15–17, Daniel 9:24).

k) The Great Commission

The Great Commission and its ministerial off-shoots (Mark 16:15-20, Matthew 28:19–20). We are called to share the Gospel with all nations and make disciples of Jesus Christ.
